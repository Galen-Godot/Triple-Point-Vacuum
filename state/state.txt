Triple Point Vacuum (TPV) is a toy ontology of physics designed to explore foundational concepts related to the vacuum. It assumes the vacuum is a continuous physical substrate with state, supporting deformations that propagate as waves. The substrate exhibits solid, fluid, and wave-like behaviors concurrently, known as the "triple point." 

It distinguishes between longitudinal, transverse, and hybrid wave modes, suggesting particles may correspond to stable deformations in this substrate. Time is treated as a parameter for accounting change, and causality is maintained in a preferred substrate frame. Quantum phenomena are interpreted through unresolved configurations, shared modes, and measurement as an energy exchange.

Core questions remain about particle stabilization, the emergence of mass, and observable signatures that might differentiate TPV from standard physics models. The framework's validity is not claimed, emphasizing internal coherence as it evolves.
